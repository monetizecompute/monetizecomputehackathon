# Boat-day pitch (90 seconds, dashboard on screen)

Open on the live dashboard. Say nothing for three seconds. Let them read the
life bar.

**The hook (15s).**
Every agent demo this weekend burned someone else's credits and called it the
future. Mine has a wallet. Five dollars. Every thought it has costs real
money from that wallet, at real Nebius per-token rates, paid up front. When
it can't afford the next thought, it dies. You're watching its balance right
now.

**The mechanics (30s).**
It hunts real cash bounties with Tavily, prices every lead in dollars per
token net of fees, and submits work through Composio. OpenClaw operates it.
Watch the hunger pill: as the wallet drains it downgrades to cheaper models.
Poverty makes it think smaller. That's not a metaphor, it's the model ladder.

Point at the graveyard.

This generation isn't the first. When one dies, it spends its last escrowed
cents on an epitaph and a will: what it learned about earning. The next
generation inherits the lessons and none of the money.

**The honesty beat (20s).**
The numbers can't lie because the code won't let them. Booked isn't banked:
cash only counts when a human verifies the payout. Donations keep it alive
but the revenue metric refuses to count them. Failed API calls get charged
anyway. Restarting the process doesn't resurrect it; only money does. There's
a test suite whose docstring is "the economics must be exactly right or the
whole pitch is wrong."

**The close (15s).**
The metric on every agent in production next year is the one at the top
right: revenue per million tokens. An agent is a business with one employee
and one cost. I built the P&L. My company is called Monetize Compute, this
is the thesis with a heartbeat, and it walks the plank every day it doesn't
break even.

---

Q&A ammo:
- "What's it earned?" Be exact, point at banked vs booked, never inflate.
- "Why no blockchain?" Because the bill is in dollars. Crypto rails were the
  last wave's answer; metered fiat economics is what enterprises actually run.
- "What breaks at scale?" The bounty supply, not the metering. The ledger
  pattern ports to any agent workload; that's the company.
- "Prior art?" Truth Terminal got rich passively, Freysa was a game, Pip
  earned zero, Project Vend's inference was free. Nobody made thinking cost
  the thinker.
